Data Visualization: Blood Glucose Levels and Diabetes

Objective:

  • Show the relationship between blood glucose levels and diabetes diagnosis.
  • Use density plots for better visualization.
ggplot(clinical_data, aes(x = Glucose, y = BloodPressure, color = factor(Outcome))) +
  geom_point(alpha = 0.7) +
  labs(
    title = "Blood Glucose vs Blood Pressure (Colored by Diabetes Status)",
    x = "Blood Glucose Level",
    y = "Blood Pressure",
    color = "Diabetes (0 = No, 1 = Yes)"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

Plot Explanation

What the Plot Shows:

–Non-diabetic patients (Outcome = 0) are more evenly spread across different blood glucose levels. Diabetic patients (Outcome = 1) are clustered in the high glucose range, indicating a strong association between high blood glucose and diabetes. There is some overlap in BP values between the two groups, but diabetic patients tend to have higher median BP levels.

Visualization Best Practices Applied:

✔ Applied transparency (alpha = 0.7) to avoid overplotting.
✔ Clear labels and title (labs()).
✔ Minimalist theme (theme_minimal()) to enhance readability.
✔ Color coding by diabetes status to differentiate groups.

Conclusion

This analysis shows a strong relationship between blood glucose levels and diabetes diagnosis. Future analysis can: - Explore other factors influencing diabetes (e.g., age, BMI, blood pressure). - Use machine learning models to predict diabetes risk. - Analyze treatment effectiveness based on patient history.
